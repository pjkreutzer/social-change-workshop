# A workshop to bring together social change researchers and activitsts in Lund

Wide spread changes to our society are needed to meet the Sustainable Development Goals.
The big question is: Will we be able to change in time?

Luckily the urgency of our situation is becoming obvious to increasingly many people.
But as sustainability topics rise in the public agenda, so does resistance to change.

Desperate activists glue themselves to buildings, goal posts in soccer stadiums and streets, hoping to increase the pressure on political decision makers.
But are these methods efficient, or are there better ways to advocate for sustainable change?
Should activism aim to reduce public resistance, or does this polarization help to keep the topic on top of the public agenda?

It is time to not only work towards goals, but also on the way in which they can be reached.
A level headed perspective from research could help make activistic idealism more effective and efficient.

Let's bring together (early career) researchers with sustainability activists in the region for a workshop on social change.
Let us work together on understanding how social change happens at various levels from persuading individuals to democratic change at supra-national levels.
If you are a researcher and want to get involved in this project please drop me a line at [philipp_jonas.kreutzer@ekh.lu.se](philipp_jonas.kreutzer@ekh.lu.se).
